Metal Gear- D

Metal Gear ZEKE, Metal Gear Sahelanthropus, and its prototype TX-55 Metal Gear preceded Metal Gear D, making it the fourth Metal Gear. Dr. Drago Pettrovich Madnar was thrown out of the scientific community and written off by the west as a radical madman three years after the Outer Heaven Uprising. Madnar, having nowhere else to go, voluntarily joined Zanzibar Land to assist in the creation of the new Metal Gear.

Metal Gear-REX

Metal Gear REX was a Metal Gear prototype created by DARPA, ArmsTech, and Livermore National Labs for the US Army in the early 2000s. In the early 1960s, Soviet scientist Aleksandr Leonovitch Granin designed a bipedal tank resembling the REX. However, Colonel Volgin of the GRU rejected his proposals in favor of Nikolai Stepanovich Sokolov's Shagohod. Drunk and resentful, Granin transmitted his plans to an American colleague, who revealed them to CIA officer Naked Snake during Operation Snake Eater. Later, triple spy Ocelot stole duplicate plans and delivered them to his CIA bosses. Since 1964, the Soviets have been researching means to launch an undetectable missile.

Metal Gear-RAY

Metal Gear RAY was an amphibious Metal Gear model that was originally meant to chase down Metal Gear REX equivalents. Although the model lacked the ability to launch nukes, it was classified as a Metal Gear since it was developed to fight REX variants that did have the ability to launch nukes but did so under the guise of nuclear strategy.

Metal Gear-Sahelanthropus

ST-84 Developed in the 1980s, Metal Gear, also known as Sahelanthropus, was a bipedal tank armed with nuclear weapons. Its production was supervised by the Soviet Union and Huey Emmerich and was used by XOF. Its primary characteristic was being able to walk and stand erect, and the name Sahelanthropus, an extinct proto-hominid species, was taken from it.
